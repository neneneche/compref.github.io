1. Technical Requirements

  • To connect your device directly into the correct network port on the wall, your device need to have an ethernet port. The network cable can be purchased at the compref if available (5m in length for EUR 4.-)
  • Devices without ethernet port need an USB to LAN adapter to be used wired.
  • There's no general WI-FI in dorm besides the one in pano lobby. If you want Wi-Fi in your room, you need to get a router. It can be purchased at the compref if available and costs EUR 20.

2. Registration Process

  • In order to register your device (Laptop, Desktop, Router, Xbox, PS etc.) you need to contact the compref at Opening Hours.

    • BEWARE!

  • DSL modems will likely NOT work! Access points (one network port on the back) can be registered, but we need ALL your devices for registration.

3. After Registration

  • If connected directly into the wall, set up your device so that it gets its IP address automatically. (DHCP - Usually this is the default setting)
  • If your computer gets infected you are likely to be banned from the network. In this case contact the compref.
  • We have an upload limit of 40GBs in 24hrs and 80GBs in 72hrs. In DOUBLE ROOMS this limit is SHARED with your roommate!!
  • To prevent overtraffic, you need to turn off automatic backups. Also, overtraffic can be caused by such things as: streaming on twitch/in discord, watching illegal streams, use of the torrents. If you get over the limit, your internet will be blocked for 72 hours! You can check your upload at http://www.panorama.sth.ac.at/ under My Account.
  • THE COMPREF CAN NOT UNBLOCK YOU IN CASE OF OVERTRAFFIC!!!

HOW TO CONNECT A ROUTER

  • In your room, please plug BOTH power and network cable at the back of your router. Network cable should be plugged into the BLUE port of the router.
  • Plug the routers' power brick into the power outlet. The network cable should be connected into 1 of 2 network ports on the wall. Correct port SHOULD be labeled with your room number. If there's no label, it's most likely the left port.
  • IF connected correctly, the LED's on the front panel of the router should be green, NOT orange or red.
  • You can find your Wi-Fi name and password at the bottom of the router.
  • Remember: WIRED connection is FASTER and more STABLE.
